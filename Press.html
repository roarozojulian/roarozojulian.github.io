I write opinion pieces for Econometría Consultores, mostly published in La República, a Colombian economic newspaper. These pieces deal with the economic outlook of Colombia and Latin America. Some, more closely tied to my research, have also appeared in Vox-EU.

Confidence crisis?

August 25, 2023

The Colombian economy is converging to its pre-pandemic trend faster than expected, yet investment (gross fixed capital formation) has fallen roughly 10%, led by declines in "Other buildings and structures" and "Housing." This drop is driven primarily by a confidence crisis and elevated uncertainty — reflected in stock market volatility and Fedesarrollo's negative confidence indices — rather than by restrictive monetary policy, as Brazil's case illustrates. To reverse this trend, boosting fiscal policy, building consensus around reforms with relevant stakeholders, combating insecurity, and reviving programs like Mi Casa Ya alongside infrastructure investment is needed.

Is the economy boosted by wages or profit?

June 16, 2023

The labor reform debate in Colombia centers on whether raising wages will increase informality and destroy jobs — as critics and a Banco de la República study suggest (estimating around half a million jobs lost) — or whether higher wages will boost aggregate demand, production, and employment, as Keynesian defenders argue. Using Goodwin's Marxist cycle model and its extension by Bhaduri and Marglin, the key question is whether Colombia's economy is profit-led (where higher wages hurt investment) or wage-led (where higher wages stimulate demand), and both graphical evidence and the majority of academic studies — including the most recent and robust one — indicate Colombia is a profit-led economy, lending support to the critics' predictions. However, this doesn't necessarily mean the reform should be scrapped; rather, it should be paired with a tax reform that offloads costs from businesses (offset by higher personal taxation) to preserve corporate profits and investment, or alternatively be redesigned in collaboration with the private sector and academia.
