I write opinion pieces for Econometría Consultores, mostly published in La República, a Colombian economic newspaper. These pieces deal with the economic outlook of Colombia and Latin America. Some, more closely tied to my research, have also appeared in Vox-EU.

La República

¿Crisis de confianza?

August 25, 2023

The Colombian economy is converging to its pre-pandemic trend faster than expected, yet investment (gross fixed capital formation) has fallen roughly 10%, led by declines in "Other buildings and structures" and "Housing." I argue that this drop is driven primarily by a confidence crisis and elevated uncertainty—reflected in stock market volatility and Fedesarrollo's negative confidence indices—rather than by restrictive monetary policy, as Brazil's case illustrates. To reverse this trend, I recommend boosting fiscal policy, building consensus around reforms with relevant stakeholders, combating insecurity, and reviving programs like Mi Casa Ya alongside infrastructure investment.
